The Da Vinci Code
by Dan Brown (2003)
Genre: Mystery / Thriller

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

ABOUT THIS BOOK

"The Da Vinci Code" is a gripping mystery thriller that begins with the murder of Jacques Saunière, the curator of the Louvre Museum in Paris. Harvard symbologist Robert Langdon is summoned to the scene, where he discovers a series of cryptic clues hidden in the works of Leonardo da Vinci. Alongside French cryptologist Sophie Neveu — Saunière's granddaughter — Langdon embarks on a frantic quest to decode the messages before a shadowy organization can silence them forever.

The novel weaves together art history, religious symbolism, and conspiracy theory into a breathless narrative that spans Paris, London, and the Scottish countryside. At its heart lies a secret that, if revealed, could shake the very foundations of Christianity. Brown's meticulous research into historical art and religious texts creates a layered puzzle that keeps readers turning pages late into the night.

With over 80 million copies sold worldwide, "The Da Vinci Code" became a cultural phenomenon, sparking debates about religion, history, and the boundaries between fact and fiction. It remains one of the best-selling novels of all time and has inspired a major motion picture franchise.

KEY THEMES

1. **The Intersection of Faith and History** — The novel challenges readers to consider how religious institutions have shaped historical narratives, and whether accepted truths may conceal deeper secrets buried for centuries.

2. **Symbolism and Hidden Knowledge** — Da Vinci's artwork serves as a canvas of concealed messages. The theme explores how symbols carry power across generations and how knowledge can be encoded in plain sight.

3. **The Sacred Feminine** — Brown explores the historical suppression of feminine divinity in Western religion, arguing that goddess worship was deliberately erased by patriarchal institutions.

4. **Power and Secrecy** — From the Priory of Sion to Opus Dei, the novel examines how secretive organizations wield influence and how the desire to control information drives human conflict.

5. **The Nature of Truth** — Throughout the story, characters grapple with what constitutes truth — whether it is found in faith, evidence, or personal belief.

ABOUT THE AUTHOR

Dan Brown (born 1964) is an American author best known for his thriller novels featuring the character Robert Langdon. Before becoming a novelist, Brown was an English teacher and musician. His breakthrough came with "The Da Vinci Code," which spent over two years on the New York Times bestseller list. His other works include "Angels & Demons," "The Lost Symbol," "Inferno," and "Origin." Brown's novels are celebrated for their fast pacing, intricate puzzles, and the seamless blending of fact and fiction.
