ABOUT THIS BOOK

"Me Before You" is a deeply moving and emotionally complex novel by Jojo Moyes that explores love, disability, quality of life, and the choices we make for those we care about. The story follows Louisa "Lou" Clark, a quirky, working-class young woman in a small English town who has never traveled far or dreamed big. When she loses her job at a café, she takes a position as a caretaker for Will Traynor, a wealthy, adventurous young man who became a quadriplegic after a motorcycle accident.

Will is bitter and withdrawn, having lost the life he once loved — extreme sports, travel, high-powered business deals. He resents his dependence and has secretly given his parents six months before he plans to end his life through assisted suicide at Dignitas in Switzerland. Lou, unaware of this timeline at first, is determined to show Will that life is still worth living.

As Lou and Will spend more time together, a deep bond forms between them. Lou challenges Will to experience joy again, while Will pushes Lou to expand her horizons and pursue a life beyond her small-town existence. The novel builds toward a devastating climax that forces both characters — and readers — to confront impossible questions about love, autonomy, and what it means to truly live.

KEY THEMES

1. Quality of Life and Autonomy — The novel bravely explores the right of individuals to make decisions about their own lives, even when those decisions are heartbreaking to loved ones.

2. Love as Transformation — Both Lou and Will are fundamentally changed by their relationship. Lou discovers ambition and courage she never knew she had, while Will finds unexpected moments of happiness.

3. Class and Ambition — Lou's working-class background and limited aspirations contrast sharply with Will's privileged former life, highlighting how circumstance shapes our expectations.

4. Disability and Perception — The novel challenges assumptions about disability, showing both the frustrations Will experiences and the full emotional life he still leads.

5. Sacrifice and Selflessness — The story asks whether love means supporting someone's choices even when you profoundly disagree with them.

CHAPTER OVERVIEW

Chapters 1-4: We meet Louisa Clark — cheerful, fashion-eccentric, and aimless — as she loses her café job and reluctantly accepts a position caring for Will Traynor. Will is hostile and dismissive, and Lou struggles to connect with him.

Chapters 5-9: Lou gradually breaks through Will's defenses with her stubborn optimism and humor. She learns about his accident and the vibrant life he once led. Their conversations grow deeper and more meaningful.

Chapters 10-14: Lou discovers Will's plan to go to Dignitas. Devastated, she creates a bucket list of experiences to convince him that life is worth living — concerts, horse races, holidays, and adventures designed to reawaken his passion for life.

Chapters 15-19: The adventures bring Lou and Will closer together. Lou begins to fall in love with Will, and there are signs that he feels the same. Meanwhile, Lou's own life begins to expand as Will encourages her to pursue her potential.

Chapters 20-24: Despite the joy they share, Will remains resolute in his decision. Lou must confront the painful reality that her love may not be enough to change his mind.

Chapters 25-Epilogue: The novel reaches its devastating conclusion. Will follows through with his plan, but he leaves Lou a legacy that transforms her life — the resources and the courage to finally live boldly and without limits.

WHY READ THIS BOOK?

"Me Before You" is not a typical love story. It is a novel that will make you laugh, cry, and question your fundamental beliefs about life, love, and autonomy. Jojo Moyes crafts characters so real and relationships so nuanced that the story's emotional impact is overwhelming. It challenges readers to sit with uncomfortable questions and resist easy answers.

This book will change the way you think about what it means to live fully and what it means to love someone enough to respect their deepest wishes, even when those wishes break your heart.

ABOUT THE AUTHOR

Jojo Moyes is a British novelist and journalist. Born in 1969 in London, she worked for ten years at The Independent newspaper before becoming a full-time author. "Me Before You" became an international bestseller and was adapted into a 2016 film starring Emilia Clarke and Sam Claflin. Moyes has written numerous bestselling novels, and she is one of only a few authors to have had simultaneous number-one bestsellers in both the US and UK. She followed this novel with two sequels: "After You" and "Still Me."
